6. Behavioral Advertising & Cookies

Explanation of Tracking Technologies and Their Purposes

Behavioural advertising refers to the practice of targeting ads to users based on their online behaviour, such as the websites they visit, the content they view, and the searches they conduct. This allows us to display ads that are more relevant and tailored to individual users' interests.

Cookies are small text files that are stored on a user's device when they visit a website. These files can store various types of information, ranging from user preferences to data used for tracking user behaviour.

  • Session Cookies: These are temporary cookies that are deleted once the user closes their browser. They're often used to remember user actions within a single session.
  • Persistent Cookies: These cookies remain on the user's device for a set period or until they're manually deleted. They're used to remember user preferences or actions across multiple sessions.
  • First-party Cookies: Issued by the website the user is visiting. They often store user preferences or session data for that specific website.
  • Third-party Cookies: Issued by a different website than the one the user is visiting. They're often used for tracking and advertising purposes across multiple websites.

Opt-out Options for Users

If you prefer not to have your online behaviour tracked for advertising purposes, you have several opt-out options:

  • Browser Settings: Most modern browsers allow users to block third-party cookies or all cookies. You can adjust these settings in your browser's privacy options.
  • Ad Network Opt-outs: Platforms like the Network Advertising Initiative (NAI) or the Digital Advertising Alliance (DAA) offer opt-out tools for users who do not want their data used for targeted advertising.
  • Do Not Track: Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" feature that sends a signal to websites requesting not to be tracked. While not all websites honour this request, Kaizen Tech respects and adheres to these signals.
  • Direct Opt-out: If you've subscribed to our newsletters or marketing communications, each email will have an "unsubscribe" link at the bottom, allowing you to opt out of future communications.
